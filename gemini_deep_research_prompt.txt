GEMINI DEEP RESEARCH PROMPT

CONTEXT:
I am a data engineer / researcher building a decision-support tool for a government innovation agency (ALC — Ireland's innovation fund, similar to a social innovation grant-maker). I have built a full pipeline that:

1. Extracts stakeholder engagement data from the KTool platform (a sensemaking / participatory engagement tool) for ~6 years of consultations, workshops, and listening exercises across mental health, youth services, community development, and social innovation funding.

2. Builds a heterogeneous network graph where nodes = agents (organisations, community groups), initiatives (projects, pilots, prototypes), perceptions (themes/opinions expressed in workshops), challenges, information items, channels (media, reports), values, patterns. Edges = relationships extracted from the data: who leads what, who partners with whom, which perceptions connect to which initiatives, which channels carry which narratives, plus interconnection edges between initiatives.

3. Runs a full analytical pipeline producing:
   - Centrality metrics (degree, betweenness, closeness, PageRank)
   - Articulation points, bridge edges, k-core decomposition
   - Robustness/stress testing (targeted vs random node removal decay)
   - Bridge scores for agents and initiatives
   - Vulnerability/fragility analysis
   - Narrative diffusion metrics
   - Semantic betweenness
   - Perception diagnostics (coherence, purity, source entropy)
   - Financial analysis for a synthetic enriched dataset (value-for-money scatter, stranded asset detection, budget reallocation simulation via Monte Carlo)
   - NLP pipeline: TF-IDF + SentenceTransformers to extract semantic relationships between quotes (similarity, contradiction, causality, sequence), cluster quotes into narrative profiles, extract subject-verb-object claim triples via spaCy
   - GNN: R-GCN trained for link prediction (predicting what connections are likely but don't yet exist between agents, initiatives, perceptions), with intervention simulation measuring PageRank shifts, component merging, information reach expansion
   - Gephi exports with filtered views

4. Visualises everything in a Streamlit dashboard for non-technical decision-makers.

THE QUESTION:
I want to investigate whether structural change is POSSIBLE within this ecosystem, from an informed political science perspective. Not "what should change?" but "given the current relational structure, what kinds of change are even feasible?" Specifically:

A) LEVERAGE — Where in the network would a small intervention have outsized structural effects? (e.g., which nodes, if strengthened or connected, would restructure the most)

B) PLASTICITY — Does the network have the capacity to accept new connections? Or is it saturated/rigid?

C) BLOCKAGES — What structural features prevent change from propagating? (isolated components, fragile articulation points, locked-in dense cores, perceptions disconnected from information sources)

D) PATH DEPENDENCY — Is the network locked into its current configuration by a dense core of well-connected actors? Or is it open to reconfiguration?

E) INTERVENTION TYPOLOGY — What kinds of interventions are structurally possible vs futile? (adding links, removing nodes, strengthening bridges, merging components)

I already have networkx-based metrics computed (above). What I lack is the POLITICAL SCIENCE frame — theories, analogies, frameworks from political network analysis, institutional change theory, advocacy coalition frameworks, or social movement studies that could help me interpret these structural metrics in a governance/decision-making context.
