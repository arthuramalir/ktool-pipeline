I've made serious progress turning the KTool data into a practical decision-making tool. Here's what I've built and what I'm thinking about next.

WHAT I'VE DONE

The core idea: we have 6+ years of stakeholder engagement data (workshops, consultations, listening exercises) from KTool. I've built a pipeline that turns all of this into a network map — who works with whom, what themes people are talking about, how information flows, where the money goes.

The pipeline:
- Extracts everything from KTool and builds a graph (~400 nodes, ~600 connections)
- Runs structural analysis: who are the most connected actors? Where are the weak points? What would happen if key people left?
- NLP on the listening data: identifies story clusters, extracts quotes, maps how different stories relate (agree, contradict, cause/effect)
- Graph Neural Network that predicts connections that DON'T YET EXIST but are likely — a kind of "what if" engine
- Financial analysis on a synthetic (fake but realistic) dataset to test budget allocation questions

THE DASHBOARD

I built a Streamlit dashboard that puts all of this in one place — network maps, story clusters, stress tests, budget analysis. It's designed for non-technical decision-makers (I stripped out all the jargon).

To run it:
1. Clone the repo: https://github.com/arthuramalir/ktool-pipeline
2. `pip install -r requirements.txt`
3. `streamlit run src/app.py`

Or I can deploy it to Streamlit Cloud and send you a link — whichever is easier.

WHAT I'M THINKING ABOUT NEXT

The deeper question I keep coming back to: given this network structure, is change even possible? I have all these analytical layers — who's central, what stories are dominant, where the blockages are — but I want to move from describing the network to understanding what kinds of structural change are feasible.

Specifically:
- Where would a small intervention have the biggest effect?
- What's blocking change from propagating?
- Is the network locked into its current configuration?

I'm starting to research this from a political science angle (network theory of policy change, advocacy coalitions, path dependency) and I was wondering: do you have any resources, reading recommendations, or contacts working on similar questions that you'd be willing to share? Even a pointer in the right direction would be hugely helpful.
